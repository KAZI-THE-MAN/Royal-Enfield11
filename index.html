Frequently Asked Questions

Feel free to ask anything

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

You may need a new helmet if it’s over 5 years old, has visible cracks, dents, frayed straps, or was involved in a crash. Faded padding or a loose fit also indicate it’s time for a replacement.

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

If your bike has been in storage, inspect it carefully before riding. Check the tires, brakes, chain, and frame for damage or rust. Ensure everything functions properly, and consider a professional tune-up if it’s been stored for a long time.

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

When riding your bike, always wear a helmet, follow traffic laws, use hand signals, and stay visible with lights or reflective gear. Ride in bike lanes when available and stay alert to your surroundings, including cars and pedestrians.
